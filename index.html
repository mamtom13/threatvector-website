// Methodology

REPORTING STANDARDS

STD-001
EVIDENCE-FIRST REPORTING
All domain abuse reports must be accompanied by verifiable, timestamped technical evidence. Assertions without supporting artifacts are rejected at triage — no exceptions.
REQUIRED
STD-002
DNS ABUSE SCOPE DEFINITION
We operate within ICANN's five DNS abuse categories: malware, botnets, phishing, pharming, and spam enabling the above. Content disputes outside this scope are not processed.
REQUIRED
STD-003
MULTI-SOURCE CORROBORATION
High-confidence reports require corroboration from at least two independent sources: sandbox analysis, passive DNS records, malware artifacts, OSINT, or network telemetry.
REQUIRED
STD-004
FALSE POSITIVE MANAGEMENT
All indicators are validated against known-good databases and threat intel feeds. Suspected false positives trigger a secondary review before any escalation to registrar or registry.
REQUIRED
STD-005
RESPONSIBLE DISCLOSURE
We follow responsible disclosure norms. Where operational security permits, affected parties are notified before public disclosure. Active law enforcement investigations take priority.
REQUIRED
STD-006
STRUCTURED EVIDENCE PACKAGING
Reports follow a standardized format: domain, abuse type, observed behavior, IOCs, evidence links, timestamps, and requested action — formatted for direct abuse desk intake.
REQUIRED
STD-007
PASSIVE DNS INTEGRATION
Historical DNS resolution data is incorporated to establish domain-IP relationships over time, identify infrastructure reuse, and detect fast-flux or bulletproof hosting patterns.
ENHANCED
STD-008
THREAT INTEL CORRELATION
Indicators are cross-referenced against public and commercial threat intelligence sources to identify campaign attribution, actor TTPs, and infrastructure reuse patterns.
ENHANCED

// Who We Are

ABOUT

ThreatVector Intelligence is a specialist DNS abuse investigation and reporting organization focused on evidence-based identification of malicious internet infrastructure.

We operate at the intersection of DNS abuse analysis, malware infrastructure research, and responsible disclosure — bridging the gap between threat identification and effective registrar and registry mitigation.

Our methodology is built on a single principle: every report must be defensible. We do not submit abuse reports based on suspicion, reputation signals, or content concerns. We investigate, validate, correlate, and document — then report with complete evidence packages that abuse desks can act on immediately.

We work within the five DNS abuse categories as defined by ICANN: malware distribution, botnet C2 infrastructure, phishing, pharming, and spam used to propagate the above. We distinguish DNS infrastructure abuse from content disputes and do not process the latter.

All work is conducted under responsible disclosure principles. Where intelligence permits and operational security allows, we coordinate disclosures with affected registrars, registries, CERTs, and law enforcement before any public release.
