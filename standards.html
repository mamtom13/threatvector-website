REPORTING STANDARDS

STD-001
EVIDENCE-FIRST REPORTING
All domain abuse reports must be accompanied by verifiable, timestamped technical evidence. Assertions without supporting artifacts are rejected at triage.
REQUIRED
STD-002
DNS ABUSE SCOPE DEFINITION
We operate within ICANN's five DNS abuse categories: malware, botnets, phishing, pharming, and spam enabling the above. Content disputes are not processed.
REQUIRED
STD-003
MULTI-SOURCE CORROBORATION
High-confidence reports require corroboration from at least two independent sources: sandbox analysis, passive DNS records, malware artifacts, OSINT, or network telemetry.
REQUIRED
STD-004
FALSE POSITIVE MANAGEMENT
All indicators are validated against known-good databases and threat intel feeds. Suspected false positives trigger secondary review before any escalation.
REQUIRED
STD-005
RESPONSIBLE DISCLOSURE
We follow responsible disclosure norms. Where operational security permits, affected parties are notified before public disclosure.
REQUIRED
STD-006
STRUCTURED EVIDENCE PACKAGING
Reports follow a standardized format: domain, abuse type, observed behavior, IOCs, evidence links, timestamps, and requested action for direct abuse desk intake.
REQUIRED
STD-007
PASSIVE DNS INTEGRATION
Historical DNS resolution data is incorporated to establish domain-IP relationships over time and detect fast-flux or bulletproof hosting patterns.
ENHANCED
STD-008
THREAT INTEL CORRELATION
Indicators are cross-referenced against public and commercial threat intelligence sources to identify campaign attribution and actor TTPs.
