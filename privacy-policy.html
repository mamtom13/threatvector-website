PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated: March 2024
Overview

ThreatVector Intelligence is committed to protecting the privacy of individuals who interact with our platform. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights. By using this website, you agree to the practices described here.

Information We Collect

Abuse report submissions: When you submit a report, we collect the information you voluntarily provide — name, organization, email, domain names, IPs, and evidence. This is used solely to process the reported abuse.

Lookup tools: URLs, file hashes, and domain names submitted to our tools are forwarded to third-party services (VirusTotal, RDAP providers) for analysis. We do not log or retain these queries.

Server logs: Standard infrastructure logs (IP addresses, browser type, timestamps) may be collected for security and operational purposes.

How We Use Your Information

Information from abuse reports is used to investigate DNS abuse, prepare evidence packages for registrars and registries, and follow up with reporters when needed. We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information to any third party.

Information Sharing

In processing abuse reports, information may be shared with registrars, registries, CERT/CSIRT teams, or law enforcement as necessary. We share only the minimum information required. Reporter identity is treated with discretion; anonymous reporting is supported where possible.

Data Retention

Abuse report submissions are retained as long as necessary for investigation accountability. Lookup queries are not stored beyond the duration of the immediate request.

Cookies

This website does not use tracking cookies, advertising cookies, or third-party analytics.

Third-Party Services

This site integrates with: VirusTotal (URL/hash analysis), RDAP/WHOIS services (domain data), Google Fonts (typography), and Cloudflare (hosting and proxy). Each operates under its own privacy policy.
