📧 SNOWSHOE SPAM

SNOWSHOE SPAM

SEVERITY: MEDIUM
What is Snowshoe Spam?

Snowshoe spam distributes spam across a large number of domains and IP addresses to minimize the volume per sending point, evading reputation-based filters. When used to distribute malware, phishing links, or fraudulent content, it constitutes DNS abuse.

How it works

Operators register hundreds or thousands of domains and IPs, sending small volumes from each to stay below detection thresholds. Despite low per-domain volume, aggregate campaigns can deliver millions of messages. WHOIS data often links all domains to a single registrant.

Key Indicators

▸ Large numbers of domains registered in bulk with similar WHOIS data
▸ Shared nameservers, registrant email, or billing address across domains
▸ Low message volume per sending IP but high aggregate volume
▸ Domains used briefly then abandoned (high churn rate)
▸ Sending IPs distributed across multiple ASNs and geographies
▸ Message content linking to malware, phishing, or fraud domains

How to Report

Document the sending domains, associated IPs, shared WHOIS data linking the campaign, message samples, and aggregate volume data. WHOIS history correlation across the domain cluster is the strongest evidence.
