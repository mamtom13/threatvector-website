PHISHING DOMAINS

SEVERITY: HIGH
What is Phishing?

Phishing is the fraudulent use of domains to impersonate legitimate organizations and deceive users into surrendering credentials, financial information, or personal data. DNS abuse enables phishing through typosquatting, homograph attacks, and lookalike domains.

How it works

Attackers register domains that visually resemble legitimate brands (e.g. "paypa1.com", "secure-bankname-login.com") and host credential harvesting pages. Phishing campaigns typically rotate domains every 24–72 hours to evade blocklists.

Key Indicators

▸ Typosquatted domains (character substitution, addition, or omission)
▸ Domains containing brand names as subdomains (e.g. paypal.attacker.com)
▸ Homograph attacks using Unicode characters resembling Latin letters
▸ Newly registered domains with SSL certificates and login page content
▸ Domains hosted on shared hosting with multiple other phishing pages
▸ Redirect chains through multiple domains to final phishing page

How to Report

Include the full phishing URL, target brand being impersonated, screenshot of the phishing page, HTML source if available, and any email headers from the phishing lure. URLScan.io reports are particularly useful.
