BOTNET INFRASTRUCTURE

SEVERITY: CRITICAL
What is a Botnet?

A botnet is a network of compromised computers controlled by an attacker via DNS-based command infrastructure. Botnets are used for DDoS attacks, spam distribution, credential theft, ransomware deployment, and cryptocurrency mining.

How it works

Botnet operators register networks of domains that serve as C2 nodes. When one domain is taken down, bots automatically connect to the next available domain. Notable botnets include Mirai (IoT), Necurs (spam), TrickBot, and ZLoader.

Key Indicators

▸ Hundreds of domains registered in bulk with similar WHOIS data
▸ Fast-flux DNS with TTL values of 60 seconds or less
▸ Domains sharing nameservers with known malicious infrastructure
▸ IP ranges associated with known bulletproof hosting providers
▸ Domains responding to HTTP/HTTPS on non-standard ports
▸ Traffic patterns showing regular beacon intervals from infected hosts

How to Report

Document the full domain cluster, associated IPs and ASNs, observed bot traffic patterns, registration data, and passive DNS history. Infrastructure correlation across multiple domains strengthens the report significantly.
